Why dogs need special food?

The nutritional balance of the dog is different from that of different animals and also different from that of humans. There are foods that we eat that a dog can certainly eat,
but nevertheless there are foods that are foreign to the dog's body that may harm his health and even cause poisoning.
The dog's nutritional needs are all obtained from the food intended for dogs and maintain a correct and healthy balance and also clean his teeth.
Even when the dog wants us to honor him with our food, we must do this in addition to the dog's special food,
and this is also done only after consulting a veterinarian about the food that a dog can eat and the food that he is not allowed to eat.
Also, the balance of adult dogs is different from that of puppies and cats, so it is forbidden to mix the foods.
Cats and puppies need higher levels of protein than adult dogs, and damage to the amount of protein may lead to health problems. In addition to this,
special attention should be paid to the taurine component that is found in cat food and is not found in dog food.
Its absence from the cat's diet may significantly damage the cat's vision and lead to heart problems.
Therefore, one must give thought to the correct distribution of food in order to maintain a correct nutritional balance for all members of the household.



Should the food be adapted to the dog's activity?

Just like humans, a more physically active dog will need a high protein food to help build muscle as well as a higher caloric intake to maintain a normal energy level.
Accordingly, dogs who are overweight may suffer from health problems just like humans,
and they should be matched with dietary food that will correct their caloric balance and lead them to a healthy weight.
Consult the veterinarian to establish a proper diet for the dog according to its weight and activity level and thus maintain a healthy balance.



How does the size of the dog affect the choice of food?

Large and small dogs need food of a different nature or with different vitamins to help their bodies deal with problems that may arise as a result of their size.
Large dogs may suffer from joint problems which are a result of their rapid development and large size.
Therefore, food intended for large dogs includes special vitamins that control the rate of growth and coats for the cartilage of the joints to prevent such problems that may arise.
Small dogs, on the other hand, tend to be more active and therefore will consume more protein.
In addition, the small mouth needs a smaller type of granules to make it easier for them to chew.



Does the dog's age require different food?

Just as puppies need puppy food, so do adult dogs need special food.
In old age, the heart and joints weaken and low-calorie food is needed to ease them. In addition,
food rich in nutritional supplements will renew the cartilage and thus alleviate joint problems that may arise in older dogs.



The difference between lamb, salmon and chicken based foods?

Chicken-based food is the usual food for dogs, but there are dogs that respond better to salmon or lamb in terms of health or personal preference.
Different types of protein will have different physical effects that may manifest in the appearance of the fur and the amount of feces.
Different dogs will react differently to the different food bases and the selection is on the market so that we can adjust the food preferred by our dog.
In addition to this, sensitivity to chicken is common in dogs and therefore there are substitutes in the form of lamb-based food and salmon-based food,
so that the dog can maintain a correct nutritional balance and not be harmed as a result of an allergy.



In conclusion

A dog's diet is very important for its health, body condition and behavior.
A dog that eats right will be stronger, more energetic and suffer less from medical problems.
Consultation with experts in the field will help build the most appropriate nutritional balance for your dog and thus maintain his health.
